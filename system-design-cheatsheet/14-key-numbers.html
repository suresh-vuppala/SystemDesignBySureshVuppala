Back-of-Envelope Estimation

Quick math for interviews

Storage: 1M users × 1KB = 1GB · 1B users × 1KB = 1TB
Time: 1 day ≈ 100K sec · 1 year ≈ 30M sec
Traffic: 1M DAU × 10 req/day = 10M/day ÷ 100K sec = ~100 RPS avg · Peak = 3-5x
Powers of 2: 2^10=1KB · 2^20=1MB · 2^30=1GB · 2^40=1TB · 2^50=1PB
80/20 Rule: 20% of data = 80% of traffic (cache hot data)
Rule of 72: Doubling time = 72 / growth% (10%/month → doubles in ~7 months)
Cost Estimation: Compute — EC2 m5.xlarge ~$140/mo, Lambda ~$0.20/1M invocations. Storage — S3 ~$23/TB/mo, EBS ~$80/TB/mo, RDS ~$200-800/mo. Bandwidth — $0.09/GB egress (first 10TB). Redis — ElastiCache r6g.large ~$200/mo. Rule: compute > storage > bandwidth for most apps.
SLA Math: 99.9% = 8.76h downtime/yr = 43.8min/mo. 99.99% = 52.6min/yr = 4.38min/mo. Serial: A_total = A1 × A2 (0.999 × 0.999 = 0.998). Parallel: A_total = 1 - (1-A1)(1-A2) (two 99.9% = 99.9999%). Always set SLO stricter than SLA — buffer before breach.
